• On Designing and Deploying Internet-Scale Services - James Hamilton whilst at Microsoft talking about how to roll out and manage the Windows Live Services Platform.
  • Recovery-Oriented Computing (ROC): Motivation, Definition, Techniques, and Case Studies - ROC project from Berkeley that ran at at the beginning of the millenium. Crash/fail fast rather and then recover rather than trying to repair and limp along.
  • Microreboot - A Technique for Cheap Recovery - Don't always think you have to reboot the whole application or even server.
  • MTTR is more important than MTBF (for most types of F) - John Allspaw talking about why it is often more important to be able to recover quickly than to make sure that you never fail in the first place. You will never guarantee 100% uptime so focus on how to recover when things go wrong.
  • Making reliable distributed systems in the presence of software errors - Joe Armstrong thesis paper explaining the Erlang approach to error handling. Note: Things are not as simple in a distributed environment.
  • Architectural Styles and the Design of Network-based Software Architectures - Roy Fielding thesis on REST.
  • Consistent Hashing and Random Trees: Distributed Caching Protocols for Relieving Hot Spots on the World Wide Web - Consistent Hashing. The original paper.
  • Programmer's Toolbox Part 3: Consistent Hashing - Consistent Hashing explained simply.
  • Dynamo: Amazon's Highly Available Key-value Store - Amazon's seminal paper on Dynamo. The pdf that launched a thousand NOSQL startups.
  • The Byzantine Generals ProblemLamport, Shostak and Pease - problems in ensuring system wide consensus in the face of unreliable sub components.
  • The Part-Time ParliamentLamport - an archeological view on distributed consensus.
  • Paxos Made SimpleLamport - simple is still a relative term I fear.
  • Time, Clocks and the Ordering of Events in a Distributed SystemLamport - what is the meaninng of time in a distributed system?
  • Why Vector Clocks are easy - the Basho boys with a simple explanation of the problems of time in a distributed system.
  • Why Vector Clocks are hard - the Basho boys with the opposite view of time in a distributed system.
  • Fault tolerance in Tandem computer systemsJim Gray - discussing the architecture of the Tandem Non Stop systems.
  • Autopilot: Automatic Data Center OperationMicheal Isard
  • Why do computers stop and what can be done about it?Jim Gray - an early analysis of system failures. Not much has changed in the ensuing 28 years.
  • A Note on Distributed ComputingWaldo, Wyant, Wollrath, Kendall on why attempts to make distributed and local objects is a fools errand. Sadly we keep making the same assumptions (and hence mistakes).
  • Fallacies of Distributed Computing - Deutsch's classic demonstration on why distributed and local are not the same thing. Can't find a link to the original PDF.
